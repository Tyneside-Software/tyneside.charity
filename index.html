Why this → The longer story

Why I’m doing this

My ambition is to bring prosperity to the North East — somehow. I’ve been looking at my own life for the highest-leverage personal way to do that. I don’t want to give money once. I want it to multiply.

A cleaner, when things were falling apart

I’ve had rough times. When everything around me is falling apart, I sometimes struggle to prioritise the little things. At one of those points, not keeping the house clean started to cause real friction with relatives. So I hired a cleaner.

It’s one of the highest-impact supports I’ve ever had — not flashy, just practical dignity when I needed it.

After Harriet was born

When I think about when that support would have mattered most, I land on the weeks after my daughter Harriet was born. A whirlwind: everyone coming to see the baby, nobody leaving you alone, sleepless nights, no rest.

For just two hours, I want to give new parents some of that time back — to relax, and to be with their baby. That’s the welcome-home clean.

Ripple effects — not just a gift

The vision isn’t only the clean. It’s the side effects that make the impact ripple:

  • Local cleaners — a pool of new customers and paid work (we’ve started companies to make that real)
  • Field-services software — this is my real job; the programme is also a serious excuse to build excellent logistics and work-management tools
  • Multiplied money — train people, win commercial work, fund more cleans and more jobs

Start where we are — Howden Community Hub and Howden Ward. Prove it. Scale ward by ward. Discussed in that spirit with Julie Marriott (Family Gateway).

The longer personal ground

There is more under this than a single rough patch or one newborn week — childhood coping that stuck, a long self-engineered path I am only now fully seeing, and a choice to walk through the door with open eyes. That story lives on the charity site only; it is not a commercial pitch.
