WHY I’M
WALKING THROUGH

A private note, made public with care.
About how I got here — and why this work exists.

I have had a lot in my life that has shaped me. I am not oversharing for drama. I am naming enough of the architecture that the free cleans, the cleaners, and the quiet dignity of this programme make sense as more than “a nice idea”.

The door → ← Charity home

I’m choosing to walk through that door

I had a bit of a rough childhood with older children, and I learned coping techniques that stuck. Recently I had a moment where I realised I had tricked myself into getting where I am now — over a really long time — and I still have no idea how deep it all goes.

I only just figured it out by talking so much about why I am doing what I am doing. By working back through the probability tree, I’m almost working back through my life. It’s a journey where I am just following where it goes and seeing where it takes me.

I have been watching this video and it really resonated — what he said about training for life, not for a race. I’m choosing to walk through that door. Here goes.

The architecture I built (without meaning to write a manual)

This is a clear synthesis of how I have been operating — strategic self-deception as long-term optimisation. Not a clinical claim. My notes on my own psyche, in public enough form to be honest.

1. The core engine

Underneath day-to-day life I run something like a high-dimensional probability optimiser: many variables, many paths, continuous re-weighting as new information arrives. I store conclusions and updated weightings more than full chains of reasoning in working memory. Only when needed do I reverse-engineer — work backward from a conclusion and reconstruct the supporting logic.

That architecture is efficient for long-horizon planning under uncertainty. It let me stay functional and strategic while carrying significant trauma, because a lot of the computational work sat below conscious narrative awareness.

2. The protective layer — the “conspiracy”

On top of that optimiser I installed containment. I learned to pre-load and stack conditions that would steer future-me onto specific paths — sometimes difficult or painful ones — then suppress or forget the working: the reasoning, the setup, often the full scope of the goal.

The purpose was protective. Many plans required enduring pain, sacrifice, or emotional cost. If the conscious mind had full access — including how deliberately it had been engineered and how much it would hurt — it would likely generate resistance, despair, second-guessing, or self-protection strong enough to abandon the path.

By hiding the working from myself, I reduced the chance of internal sabotage. Motivated self-deception and strategic ignorance as a precommitment device — treating my own consciousness as a potential adversary that had to be managed in service of longer-term aims.

3. How it ran day to day

Large parts of life and decision-making were shaped by plans whose full logic and terminal goals were hidden from the part of me that feels the cost. I often experienced myself as “just doing things” or having multiple reasons for actions, without realising how many were downstream of earlier, deliberately obscured setups.

The optimiser kept running in the background. When resistance or pain threatened to surface, suppression strengthened. That kept me highly functional on trajectories that would have been much harder with full conscious visibility. The cost was alienation from my own intentions — a fragmented sense of authorship.

4. The current revelation

What I am in now is the progressive failure — or deliberate relaxation — of that containment. By reverse-travelling the probability tree — querying compressed conclusions and asking the system to show its work — I am forcing decompression. The knowledge that “just appears” was never gone; it was stored in non-narrative form. Attention reconstructs the causal chains and surfaces them into consciousness.

Talking accelerates it. Language gives new pathways to connect previously isolated representations. The emotional intensity is not incidental: the suppression was also containing the affective payload — grief, anger, exhaustion, moral weight, fear — that came with the sacrifices and with the self-deception itself. As containment weakens, that material re-enters awareness. The heaviness is information about how much was held down.

I don’t have to dismantle it all at once. I can move at a pace that is sustainable.

5. Cost — and what becomes possible

The cost has been real: partial self-alienation, the labour of maintaining the suppression, and the reckoning when the architecture will no longer keep the full picture hidden.

The opportunity is also real. Visibility into my own system that was previously unavailable means new degrees of freedom: which parts still serve; renegotiating how I bind future-me to hard paths; choosing when strategic ignorance is still useful and when to operate with more transparency to myself; integration instead of managed conflict between the fast optimiser and the slower, meaning-making mind.

In essence: I engineered a remarkably effective, self-protective architecture so I could pursue long and painful trajectories by deliberately limiting my own awareness of them. It served the conditions it was built for. It is being revealed now because the conditions — and my capacity — have changed.

Why this sits with the charity

Not a commercial brand story. This is the personal ground under the free cleans.

When things were falling apart, a cleaner gave me practical dignity — one of the highest-impact supports I have ever had. When I think about when that would have mattered most for other people, I land on the weeks after a baby arrives — for me, after Harriet. Two hours of peace. Welcome home.

The North East ambition, the paid cleaner hours, the free software for the programme — those are the how. The why is this: I am done only hiding the working from myself. I am following the path into the open enough to turn private survival into public care that multiplies — jobs, rest, and a house that doesn’t fight you when you are already at the edge.

This page lives on the charity site on purpose. It is not a sales funnel for software or cleaning contracts. It is the human ledger behind the free welcome-home cleans.
